Diving Requirements & Safety

Participation in dive activities at Casa Escondida is subject to the following requirements. These conditions are in place for your safety and the safety of our dive team.

Certification requirementAll divers participating in guided open-water dives must hold a valid PADI Open Water certification (or equivalent from NAUI, SSI, CMAS, or similar agencies). Proof of certification card must be presented before any dive activity commences.
Medical fitness declarationAll participants must complete a standard PADI Medical Statement prior to diving. Certain medical conditions may require a physician's clearance. This is for your safety — our staff cannot override this requirement.
Age requirementsThe minimum age for PADI Open Water diving is 10 years old. Divers under 18 must have written parental or guardian consent on file.
Non-certified guestsGuests without certification may participate in our Discover Scuba Diving (DSD) experience under direct instructor supervision, to a maximum depth of 6 metres. The same medical form requirements apply.
Dive briefingsAll divers must attend the pre-dive briefing conducted by the assigned divemaster or instructor. Guests who miss the briefing may be excluded from that dive without refund.
Divemaster authorityOur divemasters and instructors have full authority to refuse, curtail, or terminate a dive if conditions are unsafe or a guest is unfit to dive. No refund will be issued for dives terminated due to guest behavior or unsafe conduct.
IntoxicationGuests who appear to be under the influence of alcohol or controlled substances will not be permitted to dive. This is a non-negotiable safety policy.

Resort Rules & Conduct

To ensure a peaceful and enjoyable environment for all guests, the following rules apply throughout the resort property:

Quiet hoursQuiet hours are observed from 10:00 PM to 7:00 AM. Loud music, gatherings, and activities that disturb other guests are not permitted during these hours.
No outside food & beveragesOutside food and beverages are not permitted in common dining areas or beach spaces. Guests may bring personal snacks to their rooms. Please respect our restaurant and bar operations.
Smoking policySmoking is strictly prohibited in all enclosed spaces, including rooms, the restaurant, and the dive center. Designated smoking areas are at the resort perimeter. Violation incurs a cleaning fee of PHP 2,000.
Environmental responsibilityThe use of non-reef-safe sunscreen is prohibited at the resort and on all dive boats. Single-use plastics are discouraged. We ask all guests to treat the marine environment with respect.
Marine protected areasAll guests must adhere to local MPA rules, including no touching, collecting, or damaging coral, marine life, or sea floor structures. Violations may result in fines under Philippine law.
Property damageGuests are responsible for any damage caused to resort property, dive equipment, or vehicles. The cost of repair or replacement will be charged to the responsible guest.
Firearms & illegal substancesThe possession of unlicensed firearms or illegal substances on resort property is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate removal and reporting to local authorities.
PetsPets are not permitted on the resort property, with the exception of certified assistance animals with appropriate documentation.

Important: All dive participants are required to sign a full liability waiver and PADI Medical Statement before their first dive. Guests who decline to sign the required forms will not be permitted to dive, and no refund will be issued for unused dive services.

Casa Escondida will not be liable for personal injury, illness, death, loss of or damage to personal property arising from participation in dive activities or resort stays, except where such loss arises directly from our gross negligence or wilful misconduct.

We strongly recommend that all guests obtain comprehensive travel insurance, including dive accident coverage (e.g., DAN — Divers Alert Network) prior to travel. Casa Escondida does not provide dive accident insurance to guests.

Casa Escondida is not responsible for loss or theft of personal belongings including underwater cameras, dive equipment, passports, cash, or valuables. Guests are encouraged to use in-room safes where provided and to store valuables securely.

Health & Medical Conditions

Certain medical conditions may disqualify a guest from participating in dive activities. These include, but are not limited to: recent surgery, asthma, epilepsy, heart conditions, uncontrolled diabetes, and certain ear or sinus conditions. The PADI Medical Statement completed prior to diving will identify any conditions requiring physician clearance.

Casa Escondida maintains basic first-aid facilities and oxygen-equipped emergency kits on-site and on all dive boats. The nearest recompression chamber is located at the Philippine Navy Base in Cavite City, approximately 90 minutes from Anilao by road. Guests with known dive injury risk factors are strongly advised to discuss this with their physician and with our dive staff prior to booking.
